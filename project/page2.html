1. Child Pornography

Child pornography refers to the sexualized exploitation and abuse of children. Most child pornography involves sexual acts of preadolescent kids with grown men. Children are victimized during the pornographic act and are exposed on the Internet against their will. The Internet provides anonymity to these criminals and the ability to pose as a different person. The majority of child pornography websites are hosted in the United States. Most of these child pornography offenders are pedophiles. Research suggests that online child pornography offenders differ from offline offenders. Offline offenders have greater access to children than online offenders. These offenders target children who are vulnerable such as those who are unhappy or needy.

2. Phishing


The term phishing or also known as 'brand spoofing' or 'carding' stems from an analogy that scammers on the Internet are using to fish for passwords and financial data from Internet users. The main target of these phishing scams are to target bank accounts and email accocunts to be used for devious purposes. Not to mention this can lead to identity theft. Phishing is also popular among these criminal syndicates to create fake IDs, gain employment, and finance their illegal activities. Phishing scams can be difficult to detect as it often looks like it comes from a reliable email account.

3.Human Trafficking

Human trafficking is done by threat or the abuse of power to receive payments or benefits to achieve the consent of a person having control over another person, for the purpose of exploitation. Some forms of exploitation include, exploitation of the prostitution of others or other forms of sexual exploitation, forced labour or services, slavery, removal of organs and many more. Human trafficking has three stages that is (1) act, (2) means, (3) purpose. The act of human trafficking involves recruiting and transporting these people. The means is how it is done, either via the use of force, deception or abuse of power. Finally, the purpose is the exploitation of the trafficked person via sexual exploitation, labor exploitation, slavery, or harvesting organs. The Internet is very much involved in human trafficking, especially in how the victim is recruited. Social media platforms such as Facebook and Instagram are used by these syndicates to reach out and gain their trust, promising them a career as a model, an actress, or becoming the wife of a wealthy man in a desirable country!! Once these victims are picked up, the traffickers take their passports and prevent the victims from communicating with their family or friends. They literally go MIA! Female victims are usually sold in brothels, forced to make pornographic videos, including sadistic practices, or even forced to perform on live Internet sex shows. Male victims on the other hand, are often contacted through employment sites and sold as labor slaves.
