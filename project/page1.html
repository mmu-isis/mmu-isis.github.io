What is ethical hacking



Ethical hacking is the process of finding vulnerabilities in a system that an attacker, or in this case a black hat hacker, may take advantage of exploiting the vulnerable data. Ethical hackers lawfully break into systems to look for vulnerabilities and find solutions to strengthen companies' data systems. Ethical hackers think alike as Black Hat Hackers but they do it in order to predict how cyberattacks are carried out so they are able to find a way to counter cyberattacks.



Phases of hacking can be summarized in 5 phases and those are ;

  2. 2.Scanning
  3. 3.Gaining access
  4. 4.Maintaining access
  5. 5.Clearing tracks

CyberSecurity Malaysia



CyberSecurity Malaysia is the national cybersecurity specialist agency under the Ministry of Communications and Digital (KKD). The mission of this agency is to develop a safer and more resilient cyber ecosystem in Malaysia. It guards national security, economic prosperity and even social harmony through providing impactful services regarding cyberthreats.




Local Agencies


The National Cyber Security Agency (NACSA)



The National Cyber Security Agency (NACSA) was officially established in February 2017 as the national Malaysian agency for cybersecurity matters. The sole purpose of this agency is to strengthen Malaysia’s ability in facing cyberthreats by cooperating with the nation’s best experts in the cybersecurity field. NACSA also strives in implementing cybersecurity policies and strategies in this nation
